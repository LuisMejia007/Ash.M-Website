As a research assistant for Dr. Brandon Moore's study of using mosquitofish (Gambusia affinis) as a bioindicator for environmental quality, I was responsible for many tasks including collecting juvenile, male, and female specimens from our universityâ€™s domain sewage plant and local water bodies. In addition, inputting all physical data within a master Excel data sheet. Once we allocated all data, we researched various scientific literature to help us formulate an effective protocol to begin the clearing and staining specimens. We decided on clearing and staining the specimens due to clear visibility of bone and cartilage, which allows for clearer measurements on sexual dimophic traits, such as the hemal spines present in both adult female and male Gambusia and gonodopdium and anal fin on the male and female respectively. We recorded all our findings in an Excel sheet. I trained incoming research members on creating and stocking solutions, performing protocols, and inputting data in our master Excel data sheet.
We collaborated with Dr. Brian Langerhans lab from North Carolina State University
to X-ray our specimens in order for us to compare and contrast our original measuring method results, clearing and stating to X-ray. Upon analyzing all of our data we commenced in creating posters of our findings. Through our findings we were invited to regional and national conferences in two different states:

  • Southeastern Society of Integrative and Comparative Biology Conference
    at Georgia Tech inAtlanta, GA
  • Society of Integrative and Comparative Biology Conference in Portland, OR
  • Society of Integrative and Comparative Biology Conference in New Orleans, LA

There, I presented our findings: "Raised in a sewage treatment plant: Assessing sexually dimorphic skeletal elements in Gambusia affinis" and "Comparing Two Techniques of Analyzing Sexual Dimorphic Gambusia affinis Skeletal Structure: Clearing and Staining to X-Ray" at each respective conference. Furthermore, my article "Using Mosquito (Gambusia affinis) as bioindicator for environmental quality on the domain" was published in my University's Science Journal
